Shamli

Shamli is a major centre of the Sugar and Jaggery in Western Uttar Pradesh, also commonly known as the Upper Doab region. The land of Shamli is very fertile which makes this place prosperous and developed.According to the 2011 census Shamli district has a population of 13,13,650.

In Dawpar yug Lord Krishana took the way of district at the time of going to Kurukshetra war from Hastinapur and in this way he sited under the trees of Barne in Hunuman Dham Shamli and took the sweet water from the old well.Hence was named after him as “Shyama vali" later “Shyama” and finally to be known as Shamli.

How to Reach

It is situated around 100 kms from Delhi, 38 kms from Panipat and 66 kms from Saharanpur. The nearest airport from Shamli is Indira Gandhi International Airport, New Delhi.Shamli is well connected to Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, Saharanpur by roadways.Shamli station is well connected to other cities in Uttar Pradesh and India.

Center of Attraction

Hanuman Tilla, one of the most revered temples of West Uttar Pradesh, is located at historical town Shamli belongs to Shamli district in UP, India. Hanuman Tilla also called Hanuman Dham is believed to have been built by the great warrior Bhim, one of the Pandavas in great Indian epic Mahabharat.

SHAMLI

Shamli is city and district headquarter in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. This district was carved out from Muzaffarnagar District on 28 September 2011 as Prabudh Nagar and renamed Shamli in July 2012.Shamli is a part of National Capital Region (NCR) and is located along the Delhi–Saharanpur (709B), Meerut-Karnal (709A) and Panipat-Khatima (709AD) Highways.
